Introduction

Since “Putting All the Cards on the Table (2014)” was written and finalised, I kept linking to individual sections of it, and it proved to be popular and many people liked it or individual sections of it. One thing I now regret about it is the word "All" because I now realise that honesty is a process, and so is enlightenment and if you stop searching for a bigger and better “truth”, you stop being honest.

Nevertheless, after over a year, I feel that a sequel to it is in order, collecting and organising a (hopefully large) subset of the various insights I collected in many posts and insights on various social media outlets, some stories, screenplays, aphorisms, etc. So here goes.

««««««
This essay/article which someone linked to on Freenode's ##programming is a prime example of how you should write essays in this day and age:

http://brikis98.blogspot.in/2014/05/dont-learn-to-code-learn-to-think.html

Mainly it has:

1. Many images that are often relevant.

2. Short paragraphs.

3. Easily skimable text.

4. Many text embellishments such as bullets, itemised lists, bold, etc.

This is as opposed to a text with long paragraphs/etc.

This is not a new trend. Russians pride themselves on having the best 19th century and 20th century novelists, but even https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crime_and_Punishment is much less usable and popular than those of French, British and  American novelists: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twenty_Thousand_Leagues_Under_the_Sea ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Three_Musketeers ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adventures_of_Tom_Sawyer ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Little_Women ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jane_Eyre ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treasure_Island ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hobbit ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alice%27s_Adventures_in_Wonderland ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Railway_Children - all novels that aged extremely well.

Even a large part of the Jewish Bible / Tanakh / Old Testament is extremely succinct and to the point (not sure about the so-called New Testament).
